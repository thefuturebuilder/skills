# Introduction Script Patterns

These are patterns extracted from successful Future Builder course introductions. Use them as models when writing new course intros.

## Pattern 1: Luke Davies — Basecamp Module 1 (Structured/Reflective)

**Opening**: Personal introduction + metaphor that frames the entire course
- "Imagine you're on a trip and you don't know the destination. Even if you had a GPS, you'd still be lost..."
- Connects the metaphor to the audience's situation

**Problem**: Honest vulnerability about own early struggles
- "When I first started my building company, honestly, vision, mission, values — it all sounded like corporate jargon."
- Shows empathy and credibility simultaneously

**Promise**: Clear, tangible outcome with excitement
- "By the end of this module, you'll have a clear Business Map — a completed one-page guide for your business"
- Names the exact deliverable

**Journey Preview**: Explains the structure without overwhelming
- "In each of the following eleven lessons, we'll break down a specific element..."
- Mentions both parts of the Business Map (foundational + organisational)

**Asset Setup**: Practical preparation instructions
- "You will also want to download the Business Map and lesson worksheet asset attached to this module"
- Suggests note-taking approach (paper + digital mix)

**Mindset Framing**: Sets up key concepts used throughout
- Introduces "Thinking Time" as sacred
- Introduces "Operate vs. Create" mode distinction
- Introduces "Note-making vs. note-taking"

**Close**: Building metaphor + energy
- "You know building a home can seem overwhelming... But you also know the secret: chunking things down"
- Ends with enthusiasm and call to start

### Key Characteristics
- ~800-1000 words
- Multiple metaphors layered (GPS, vehicle, building blocks)
- Warm, encouraging, slightly formal but authentic
- References own businesses (Davies Construction, Future Builder)
- Gives permission to borrow/copy frameworks

## Pattern 2: David Jenyns — Critical Systems (Casual/Punchy)

**Opening**: Direct credibility + immediate value proposition
- "Hi, I'm David Jenyns, and welcome to the Critical Client Flow Blueprint."
- Gets straight to the point

**Problem**: Calls out the common mistake bluntly
- "Most business owners think they need to document every single process... They look at this massive mountain of work and they freeze."
- Empathetic but direct

**Promise**: Specific, tangible, with timeframe context
- "By the end of this course, you'll have mapped out your Critical Client Flow — a simple, one-page diagram..."
- Emphasises simplicity

**Metaphor**: Single strong metaphor that carries the whole course
- Plumbing system with leaks — "If you've got a leak, you don't rip out every pipe in the house, do you?"
- Returns to this metaphor repeatedly

**Practical Setup**: Very low-barrier
- "Eleven short lessons, each one around 3 to 5 minutes"
- "Grab a notebook or open up a notes app on your phone"
- "Version one is better than version none"

**Close**: Action-oriented, preview next lesson
- "Download that worksheet, grab your notebook, and I'll see you in the next lesson"
- Specific instruction + energy

### Key Characteristics
- ~600-800 words (shorter, punchier)
- One dominant metaphor (plumbing) used throughout
- Very casual Australian tone ("mates", "sparky", direct address)
- Real tradesperson examples (electrician, plumber, builder)
- Strong "just get it done" energy
- Less philosophical, more practical

## Pattern 3: Kyle Zanetto — Ultimate Dashboard (Industry Insider)

**Opening**: Personal pain point that resonates
- "I'm going to unveil the ultimate dashboard... that I wish I had when I started Zanetto Builders"
- "I fumbled along, never truly knowing how my business was performing"

**Problem**: Emotional rollercoaster description
- "Some days I would be on cloud nine and the next I'd be down in the dumps because the building industry along with its cash flow is a bloody roller coaster"

**Promise**: Feature-rich but outcome-focused
- Lists what the dashboard includes (income, cost of sales, overheads, gross profit, net profit)
- Shows it as dollar AND percentage values
- Mentions forecast, graphs, CRM, personal tracking

**Unique Hook**: Technology integration
- Text message every evening tracking hours worked, professional development, days off
- "This gives us your effective hourly rate... lets us determine if you're working more for less or less for more"

### Key Characteristics
- ~300-400 words (very concise)
- Feature-forward but tied to outcomes
- Authentic frustration turned into solution
- Industry-specific language (cash flow, KPIs, CRM)
- "Bloody roller coaster" — unapologetically Australian

## General Introduction Template

Use this as a starting framework, then adapt to the expert's voice:

```
[Expert Name] introduces themselves warmly

[Paint the problem — 2-3 sentences using language from the transcript]

[Promise the outcome — "By the end of this course, you'll have [specific deliverable]"]

[Brief journey preview — mention lesson count and high-level flow]

[Set up the metaphor that will carry through the course]

[Practical setup — what to download, what to have ready]

[Mindset framing — any key concepts or mantras for the course]

[Energy close — excitement + specific call to start]
```
