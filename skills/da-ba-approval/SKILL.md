# DA/BA Approval Documentation Skill

## Overview

This skill provides a comprehensive workflow for preparing Development Application (DA) and Building Application (BA) documentation for commercial and light industrial developments in Australia, with specific expertise in Tasmanian planning and building requirements.

The approval process has two distinct phases, each with different regulatory frameworks, required documentation, and assessment criteria.

## When to Use This Skill

This skill should be activated whenever the user needs help with:
- Preparing a DA or BA submission
- Understanding what documentation is required for council/building surveyor
- Generating cover pages, drawing schedules, or specification documents
- Reviewing plans against NCC/BCA or Australian Standards compliance
- Creating checklists for DA or BA submissions
- Understanding the Tasmanian Planning Scheme zone requirements
- Cross-referencing engineering codes and standards for construction documentation
- Preparing site analysis reports, traffic assessments, or stormwater management plans
- Generating WHS documentation for construction projects

## Phase 1: Development Application (DA)

### Regulatory Framework
- **Assessed under**: Tasmanian Planning Scheme (or relevant Interim Planning Scheme)
- **Submitted to**: Local Council Planning Department
- **Assessment body**: Council planners (may involve referral to TasWater, State Growth, TasNetworks)
- **Typical timeline**: 28 days (Permitted pathway) or 42 days (Discretionary pathway)

### Required Documentation Checklist

1. **Application Form** — Council-specific DA form with owner consent
2. **Certificate of Title** — Current title search (less than 3 months old)
3. **Site Plan** (1:100 or 1:200 @A1) showing:
   - Property boundaries with dimensions
   - Existing and proposed buildings with setbacks
   - Vehicle access points and parking layout
   - Stormwater management approach
   - Landscaping zones
   - North point and scale bar
4. **Floor Plans** (1:100 @A1) — All levels with room labels, dimensions, GFA calculations
5. **Elevations** (1:100 @A1) — All four sides showing materials, heights, boundary setbacks
6. **Sections** (1:50 or 1:100) — At least one cross-section and one longitudinal section
7. **Traffic Impact Assessment** (if >40 vehicle movements/day or >20 parking spaces)
8. **Stormwater Management Plan** — Pre- and post-development flows, detention/retention
9. **Bushfire Assessment** (if BAL > LOW)
10. **Landslide Risk Assessment** (if in landslide hazard area)
11. **Heritage Assessment** (if heritage listed or in heritage precinct)
12. **Environmental Impact** — Noise, dust, vibration, emissions assessment for industrial uses

### Key Tasmanian Planning Scheme Provisions

For Light Industrial zone developments, assess against:
- **Zone Purpose**: Industrial zone clauses (use standards, development standards)
- **C2.0 Parking and Sustainable Transport** — parking ratios, access width, turning circles
  - Light Industrial: 1 space per 50m² GFA (or as per table)
  - AS 2890.1 for car parking geometry
  - AS 2890.2 for commercial vehicle facilities
- **C3.0 Road and Railway Assets** — access to road network
- **C4.0 Electricity Transmission Infrastructure** — clearances from powerlines
- **E5.0 Road and Railway Crossings** — new or modified access points
- **E6.0 Car Parking and Sustainable Transport**
- **E7.0 Stormwater Management** — detention, water quality, connection to council infrastructure
- **E9.0 Attenuation** — buffer distances for industrial activities
- **E10.0 Biodiversity** — if native vegetation affected
- **E15.0 Inundation Prone Areas** — flood mapping check

### Assessment Pathways
- **No Permit Required**: Minor works meeting all Acceptable Solutions
- **Permitted**: Meets all Acceptable Solutions for the zone and applicable codes
- **Discretionary**: Requires assessment against Performance Criteria (may require public notification and 14-day representation period)

## Phase 2: Building Application (BA)

### Regulatory Framework
- **Assessed under**: National Construction Code (NCC) 2022 / Building Code of Australia (BCA)
- **Submitted to**: Building Surveyor (council-appointed or private)
- **Assessment body**: Registered Building Surveyor
- **Key legislation**: Building Act 2016 (Tasmania), Building Regulations 2016

### Required Documentation Checklist

1. **BA Application Form** — Standard Building Application form
2. **Planning Permit** — Copy of approved DA (or evidence of permitted use)
3. **Owner's Consent** — Written consent from property owner

#### Architectural Drawings (A-Series)
4. **A001 Project Information** — Drawing schedule, project data, site classification, revision history
5. **A002 Site Survey** — By registered surveyor, showing boundaries, levels, services, easements
6. **A101 Site Plan** (1:100 @A1) — Building footprint, setbacks, parking, access, landscaping
7. **A102 Setout Plan** (1:100 @A1) — Dimensioned building positions from boundaries
8. **A103 Floor Plan** (1:100 @A1) — Room layouts, door/window schedules, areas
9. **A104 Roof Plan** (1:100 @A1) — Roof layout, falls, gutters, downpipes
10. **A105 Lighting & Electrical Plan** — Light positions, switchboard, power points
11. **A201 Elevations** (1:100 @A1) — All elevations with materials, heights, openings
12. **A301 Sections** (1:50 or 1:100) — Key building sections showing construction method
13. **A401 Door & Window Schedule** — Sizes, types, hardware, fire ratings

#### Civil Engineering Drawings (C-Series)
14. **C200 Cover Page** — Drawing schedule, general notes, basis of design
15. **C201 Drainage/Site Plan** — Stormwater layout, pipe sizes, grades, pits, connection points
16. **C202 Pavement Plan** — Proposed pavement areas, levels, grades, crossover details
17. **C203 Cut and Fill Plan** — Earthworks volumes, elevation changes
18. **C204 Compliance Long Section** — Pipe grades, inverts, cover depths per AS/NZS 3500.3
19. **C205 Water and Sewer Plan** — Water supply, sewer connections, fire services
20. **C206 Detention Detail** — Underground detention system design, orifice sizing
21. **C207-C208 Drawing Details** — Pavement cross-sections, disabled parking, wheel stops, kerbing
22. **C209 Civil Notes** — General conditions, earthworks specs, water/sewer/stormwater requirements

#### Structural Engineering Drawings (S-Series)
23. **S200 Cover Page** — Drawing schedule, design parameters, site/wind classification
24. **S201 Slab & Footing Plan** — Slab thickness, mesh, footing layout, pour sequence
25. **S202 Blockwork/Wall Joint Plan** — Control joint locations, fire-rated sealant details
26. **S203 Roof Framing Plan** — Roof structure, bracing layout, member sizes
27. **S204 Tie-Down & Bracing Details** — Wind resistance connections per AS 1684.2
28. **S205-S208 Structural Details** — Footing sections, wall connections, slab details, tilt-up options
29. **S209-S210 Structural Notes** — Material specs, concrete/reinforcement/timber/masonry/steelwork requirements

#### Supporting Documentation
30. **Geotechnical Report** — Soil classification per AS 2870, bearing capacity, recommendations
31. **Energy Efficiency Assessment** — Section J (NCC) compliance, JV3 or DTS pathway
32. **Structural Adequacy Certificate** — Engineer's certificate for structural design
33. **Fire Safety Report** — Fire resistance levels (FRL), egress, fire services (if required)
34. **Accessibility Report** — AS 1428.1 compliance for DDA requirements
35. **WHS Plan** — Workplace Health & Safety documentation per WHS Act 2011
36. **Soil & Water Management Plan** — Erosion and sediment control during construction

### NCC Building Classifications (for Light Industrial)
- **Class 7a**: Carpark
- **Class 7b**: Storage or display of goods (warehouse)
- **Class 8**: Laboratory or factory/production facility
- **Class 10a**: Non-habitable building (shed, carport)
- **Class 10b**: Structure (fence, mast, retaining wall)

## Drawing Conventions

### Numbering System
| Prefix | Discipline | Examples |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| A | Architectural | A001, A101, A201, A301, A401 |
| C | Civil Engineering | C200-C209 |
| S | Structural Engineering | S200-S210 |
| E | Electrical | E001-E100 |
| M | Mechanical/HVAC | M001-M100 |
| P | Plumbing/Hydraulic | P001-P100 |
| L | Landscape | L001-L100 |
| SCH | Schedules/WHS | SCH01-SCH02 |

### Standard Scales
| Drawing Type | Scale @A1 | Scale @A3 |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| Site Plan | 1:100 or 1:200 | 1:200 or 1:400 |
| Floor Plan | 1:100 | 1:200 |
| Elevations | 1:100 | 1:200 |
| Sections | 1:50 or 1:100 | 1:100 or 1:200 |
| Details | 1:5 to 1:20 | 1:10 to 1:40 |

### Title Block Requirements
Every drawing must include:
- Project name and address
- Client name
- Drawing title and number
- Scale (both graphical and written)
- Revision number and date
- Designer/engineer name, registration, and contact
- North point (on plans)
- Revision history table

## Workflow Sequence

### Pre-Lodgement
1. Pre-application meeting with council (recommended)
2. Title search and planning scheme check
3. Survey commissioned (registered surveyor)
4. Geotechnical investigation
5. Service authority searches (Dial Before You Dig)

### DA Phase
6. Architectural concept design
7. Traffic impact assessment (if required)
8. Stormwater management concept
9. DA documentation package assembled
10. Lodge DA with council
11. Council assessment (28-42 days)
12. Address any Requests for Further Information (RFI)
13. Planning Permit issued with conditions

### BA Phase
14. Detailed architectural drawings (full set)
15. Structural engineering design and drawings
16. Civil engineering design and drawings
17. Energy efficiency assessment (Section J)
18. Fire safety assessment (if required)
19. WHS documentation
20. BA documentation package assembled
21. Lodge BA with Building Surveyor
22. Building Permit issued
23. Pre-construction meeting
24. Construction commences

### Post-Construction
25. Stage inspections (footings, frame, pre-lining, final)
26. As-built documentation
27. Certificate of Completion
28. Occupancy Permit (if required)

## Site Classification Quick Reference

When preparing documentation, establish these parameters early:

| Parameter | Standard | Common Values |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Soil Class | AS 2870-2011 | A, S, M, H1, H2, E, P |
| Wind Class | AS 4055-2021 | N1, N2, N3, N4, C1, C2, C3, C4 |
| BAL Rating | AS 3959-2018 | BAL-LOW, 12.5, 19, 29, 40, FZ |
| Corrosion | AS 4312 | C1-CX (atmospheric), Im1-Im4 (immersion) |
| Importance Level | AS 1170.0 | 1 (normal), 2 (post-disaster), 3 (special) |
| Seismic Class | AS 1170.4 | Based on importance level and soil type |

## Tips for Efficient Approval

1. **Start with the survey** — Everything builds from the survey data
2. **Engage early** — Pre-application meetings save time and money
3. **Check zone requirements first** — Some uses may be prohibited
4. **Coordinate disciplines** — Architect, civil, and structural must cross-reference
5. **Use consistent datums** — All drawings should reference the same survey datum
6. **Track revisions meticulously** — Every change gets a new revision number
7. **Include "Before You Dig" results** — Underground services affect every discipline
8. **Reference standards by edition** — Always cite the specific year/edition
9. **Written dimensions take precedence** — Always note this on drawings
10. **Documentation subject to statutory approvals** — Include this disclaimer
