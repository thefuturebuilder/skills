# Tasmanian Planning and Building Requirements

This reference covers Tasmanian-specific planning and building processes, applicable for Development Applications (DA) and Building Applications (BA) across Tasmania, with emphasis on North-West Coast councils (Devonport, Latrobe, Central Coast).

## 1. Tasmanian Planning Framework

### Regulatory Structure

The planning system in Tasmania is governed by:

- **Planning Act 1998 (Tasmania)** — Primary legislation for development approvals
- **Tasmanian Planning Scheme (TPS)** — Statewide planning scheme replacing various interim schemes (commenced 2023)
  - If pre-2023: Project may be assessed under Interim Planning Schemes (e.g., Interim Planning Scheme for Devonport, Latrobe)
  - Check current status with council — some areas may still operate under interim schemes during transition
- **Building Act 2016 (Tasmania)** — Primary legislation for building permits
- **Building Regulations 2016 (Tasmania)** — Technical requirements aligned with NCC 2022

### TPS Structure and Application

The Tasmanian Planning Scheme (TPS) is organized into:

#### A. General Provisions (C-Codes)
These apply to all zones and land uses across Tasmania. Assessment codes are prefixed with "C":

- **C1.0 Residential use** — Applies to residential developments
- **C2.0 Parking and sustainable transport** — Applies to all developments
  - Parking rate for Light Industrial: 1 space per 50m² GFA (or per specific table if modifying)
  - Accessible parking per AS 2890.1 / AS 1428.1
  - Bicycle parking if specified in Development Standards
- **C3.0 Road and railway assets** — Access to road network, frontage requirements
- **C4.0 Electricity transmission infrastructure** — Clearance from high-voltage lines
  - Typical clearances: 10m from distribution lines, 25m+ from transmission lines (consult TasNetworks)
- **C5.0 Hazardous materials** — Storage and handling requirements
- **C6.0 Hazard and risk** — General hazard assessment
- **C7.0 Subdivision** — For land division (if applicable)
- **C8.0 Archaeological and heritage matters** — Heritage significance assessment
- **C9.0 Special areas** — Environmental protection areas, Ramsar sites
- **C10.0 Utilities and essential services** — Water, sewer, power connections

#### E-Codes (Specific Provisions by Zone)

For **Light Industrial Zone**:

- **E1.0 Zone purpose** — Defines intended use: "Industrial and related uses"
  - Permitted uses: Manufacturing, assembly, storage, warehousing, service stations, trade suppliers
  - Prohibited uses: Residential (typically), retail sales, entertainment venues
- **E5.0 Road and railway crossings** — New or modified property access
  - Requires compliance with road safety and sight distance standards
  - For State Growth roads, refer to State Growth Access Guidelines
- **E6.0 Car parking and sustainable transport** — Zone-specific parking provisions
  - May reference AS 2890.1, AS 2890.2 for commercial vehicle access
- **E7.0 Stormwater management** — Detention and quality requirements
  - Pre- and post-development flow calculations
  - Water quality treatment (sediment, nutrients)
  - Connection to council stormwater network
  - Typical detention: Retaining post-development runoff to pre-development rates (or site requirements)
- **E9.0 Attenuation** — Buffer distances for industrial activities
  - Separation from sensitive uses (schools, hospitals, residences)
  - Noise, dust, vibration control measures
  - May require acoustic assessment, air quality modeling
- **E10.0 Biodiversity** — Protection of native vegetation
  - Vegetation offset requirements if clearing > threshold
  - Threatened species assessment
  - Habitat connectivity
- **E11.0 Scenic quality** — Landscape impact (for visible sites)
- **E12.0 Hazardous materials storage** — If applicable to industrial use
- **E13.0 Energy efficiency** — Building performance standards (Section J, NCC)
- **E15.0 Inundation prone areas** — Flood mapping compliance
  - Buildings in flood zones require floor level above flood level + freeboard
  - Typical freeboard: 0.5m-1.0m above 1% AEP (100-year) flood level
  - Check council flood mapping (available from planning department)

### Assessment Pathways

When lodging a DA, it will be assessed under one of three pathways:

1. **No Permit Required (NPR)**
   - Development meets all Acceptable Solutions in the planning scheme
   - No application needed; can proceed directly to BA
   - Rarely applies to commercial/industrial (most require DA assessment)

2. **Permitted (P)**
   - Meets all Acceptable Solutions for the zone and relevant codes
   - Assessment typically 28 days
   - No public notification
   - Conditions may be applied
   - Planning Permit issued

3. **Discretionary (D)**
   - Does not meet all Acceptable Solutions; assessed against Performance Criteria
   - Assessment typically 42 days (may extend if RFI issued)
   - Public notification required (14-day representation period)
   - Higher risk of objections or refusal
   - More detailed assessment required

## 2. Light Industrial Zone Development Standards

### Zone Definitions

**Light Industrial Zone** is intended for:
- Manufacturing and processing of goods
- Assembly and fabrication
- Warehousing and storage
- Trade suppliers and service stations
- Motor vehicle repairs
- Similar industrial uses

**Typically NOT Permitted** in Light Industrial:
- Dwelling (residential)
- Retail sales to the public (unless ancillary to primary use)
- Petrol filling stations (may be Permitted or Discretionary depending on specifics)
- Hazardous goods storage (may be Discretionary with conditions)
- Offensive trades (may be Prohibited)

### Development Standards for Light Industrial

| Requirement | Acceptable Solution | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Building height | Up to 15m (or per local code) | Measured from ground level to roof ridge |
| Setback from front boundary | 6-10m (or per local code) | Provides frontage buffer |
| Setback from side boundary | 2-3m (or per local code) | Fire safety and privacy |
| Setback from rear boundary | 2-3m (or per local code) | May increase if abutting residential zone |
| Site coverage | Up to 60% (or per local code) | Allows on-site parking/landscaping |
| Floor space ratio (FSR) | No specific limit (or per code) | Often limited by parking and setbacks |
| Parking rate | 1 space per 50m² GFA | AS 2890.1 standard; may be relaxed if justifiable |
| Accessible parking | 1 + 1 per 40 spaces | Per AS 2890.1 and AS 1428.1 |
| Vehicle access | Dedicated access from public road | Width per AS 2890.1 (min 3.5m for two-way) |
| Internal circulation | Heavy vehicles (trucks) to be accommodated | AS 2890.2 swept paths |
| Loading/unloading | Off-street provision required | Truck maneuvering within property |
| Landscaping | 10-15% of site area | Softscape, screening of views from street |
| Stormwater | Retaining pre-development flow rates | AS/NZS 3500.3 compliance |
| Waste management | Enclosed bin storage | Screened from public view |
| Signage | Limited to building frontage | Height and area per planning scheme |

**Note**: Check specific council codes (e.g., Devonport TPS, Latrobe TPS) for exact thresholds, as development standards vary.

## 3. Tasmanian Planning Scheme General Provisions (C-Codes) Summary

### C2.0 Parking and Sustainable Transport

**Acceptable Solutions:**
- Parking spaces compliant with AS 2890.1 (cars) or AS 2890.2 (commercial vehicles)
- Disabled parking per AS 2890.1: minimum dimensions and accessible route to building
- Parking layout: appropriate turn-around radii, aisles widths
- For Light Industrial: 1 space per 50m² GFA minimum (or as per table)
- Alternative: Reduced parking if public transport or car-sharing provided

**Performance Criteria (if seeking exemption):**
- Parking demand analysis showing on-street availability
- Public transport accessibility assessment
- Carpooling or car-share facilities
- Demand management plan (e.g., flexi-hours)

**Documentation Required in DA:**
- Parking layout plan (A101, C202-C203)
- Calculation: GFA x parking ratio = required spaces
- Accessible parking provision (separate plan)
- Swept path analysis for heavy vehicles (if applicable)

### C3.0 Road and Railway Assets

**Acceptable Solutions:**
- Access to land must be from an existing public road
- Frontage width sufficient for property access (typically 6m minimum)
- No access from railway lines or limited access roads
- Sight distances: 90m at 50km/h zone, 120m at higher speeds (consult with council)

**Performance Criteria:**
- Alternative access via shared rights-of-way
- Traffic signal control (if on busy road)
- Reduced sight distances in urban contexts

**Documentation Required:**
- Site plan showing existing road frontage (A101, A002 survey)
- Sightline diagram (C202 or separate traffic plan)

### C4.0 Electricity Transmission Infrastructure

**Acceptable Solutions:**
- Building must be >10m from distribution powerlines
- Building must be >25m from transmission powerlines
- Consult with TasNetworks for exact clearances

**Documentation Required:**
- TasNetworks clearance search (before commencing design)
- Note on A002 site survey if powerlines are nearby
- Notation on A101 site plan showing exclusion zones

### C5.0 Hazardous Materials (if applicable)

**If storing hazardous materials** (e.g., oils, chemicals, paints):
- Bunded storage containers per Australian Standards
- Separation from drains and waterways
- Signage and safety information
- Environmental Protection Notice (EPA compliance)

### E7.0 Stormwater Management

**Acceptable Solutions:**
- Post-development runoff ≤ pre-development runoff (1% AEP = 100-year event)
- Quality treatment: sediment removal, nutrient reduction
- Detention system design per AS/NZS 3500.3
- Connection to council stormwater network (if available)

**Calculation Methodology:**
- Rational method: Q = 0.278 × C × I × A
  - C = runoff coefficient (varies by surface: 0.9 for impervious, 0.3 for vegetated)
  - I = rainfall intensity (5-year ARI, 10-minute duration; varies by region)
  - A = catchment area (hectares)
- Pre-development: Assume current land use (agricultural, 0.3-0.4 C)
- Post-development: Industrial hard surfaces (0.85-0.95 C)

**Detention System:**
- Underground tanks, surface ponds, or combination
- Volume calculation: V = (Post-dev flow - Pre-dev flow) × Time to peak
- Discharge rate controlled by orifice sizing per stormwater code
- Overflow to existing council network (if overtopping)

**Water Quality Requirements:**
- First flush treatment (TSS removal)
- Oil/water separation (if vehicle movement likely)
- Sediment pond (for construction phase)

**Documentation Required:**
- Stormwater management plan (C200-C209)
- Pre/post calculations, detention design
- Water quality design
- Connection diagram to council network
- AS/NZS 3500.3 compliance summary

### E15.0 Inundation Prone Areas

**Flood Risk Assessment:**
- Check council flood mapping (planning department website or in-person)
- 1% AEP (100-year ARI) is standard design flood
- Some areas also have 0.5% AEP (200-year) modeling

**Acceptable Solutions (if in flood area):**
- Building slab height ≥ design flood level + 0.5m freeboard
- Freeboard varies by council: typically 0.5-1.0m
- Ground floor habitable rooms above flood level
- Mechanical plant (HVAC, electrical) above flood level
- Basement or below-slab services acceptable if mechanical closure available

**Documentation Required:**
- Flood level certificate from Building Surveyor
- Site survey showing finished floor levels vs. flood level (A002, A101)
- Cross-section showing freeboard (A301)
- Flood evacuation plan (if required)

## 4. Specific Area Plans (SAPs)

Some councils have adopted Specific Area Plans for key development areas:

### North-West Coast (Devonport, Latrobe, Central Coast Area)

**Town Centre / Commercial Precincts:**
- May have specific development standards (building height, frontage, setbacks)
- Often have heritage overlays (check for heritage assessment triggers)
- Parking requirements may be relaxed in central locations
- Public transport accessibility prioritized

**Industrial Precincts:**
- Designated industrial areas with relaxed residential setbacks
- Higher site coverage allowances
- Permit vehicle movements (trucks, forklifts)
- Buffer zones from residential areas

**Waterfront / Coastal Areas (if applicable):**
- Additional scenic quality assessments
- Vegetation protection overlays
- Setbacks from the mean high-water mark
- Aboriginal heritage assessments

**Consult local council planning department for area-specific variations.**

## 5. Common Conditions on Planning Permits

When a DA is approved, typical conditions include:

### Design and Construction Conditions
1. **Compliance with plans**: Construction must match approved plans (with minor variations only)
2. **Finishes**: Agreed external materials, colors, finishes
3. **Landscaping**: Planting plan implementation, maintenance bond
4. **Screening**: Bins, mechanical plant, utilities to be screened from street view
5. **Signage**: Limited to approved signage plan (size, position, materials)
6. **Hours of operation**: If industrial, may restrict delivery/truck movements (e.g., 6am-6pm weekdays only)

### Environmental and Traffic Conditions
7. **Stormwater**: Constructed per approved stormwater plan, tested and certified
8. **Water quality**: TSS, nutrient removal as specified
9. **Erosion control**: Sediment and erosion control plan during construction
10. **Traffic management**: Traffic control plan for construction phase, site access
11. **Parking**: Maintained at approved rate; no on-street overflow

### Statutory Approvals
12. **Building approval**: DA approval is NOT building approval; separate BA required
13. **Service authority approval**: TasWater connection, TasNetworks consent (if applicable)
14. **Aboriginal heritage**: If triggered, archaeological assessment required
15. **Environmental assessment**: EPA approval if hazardous materials, emissions, noise controls

### Operational Conditions
16. **Noise control**: Sound levels per EPA guidelines; operating hours restriction
17. **Air quality**: Dust suppression during operations, vehicle movement controls
18. **Waste management**: Waste collection times, bin storage, recycling provision
19. **Community liaison**: Regular meetings with neighbors (if contentious)
20. **Monitoring**: Ongoing compliance inspections

## 6. TasWater Requirements

For properties requiring water, sewer, or trade waste services:

### Water Supply
- **Mains connection**: If available, required to connect
- **Meter location**: Typically at boundary; TasWater specifies position
- **Water meter box**: Approved design, accessible, marked
- **Pipe sizing**: Depends on flow demand (design engineer specifies)
- **Pressure and flow**: TasWater guarantees 20 kPa minimum static pressure

### Sewer Connection
- **Gravity sewer**: If main available, required to connect
- **Sewer line**: Laid per TasWater standard drawings; min. 150mm diameter
- **Connection point**: TasWater determines via inspection (usually existing pit or new pit if main near property)
- **Sanitary fixtures**: All sanitary drains connect to main sewer (no septic tanks in sewer areas)
- **Grease trap**: May be required for food premises; approval required

### Trade Waste (for industrial uses)
- **Approval required**: For non-sanitary industrial discharge
- **Pre-treatment**: Oils, heavy metals, suspended solids must be treated
- **Trade Waste Agreement**: Formal agreement specifying discharge volumes, quality, disposal costs
- **Monitoring**: TasWater may require quarterly or annual testing

### TasWater Application Process
1. Obtain TasWater reference from council DA approval
2. Submit connection application with site plan and architectural plans
3. TasWater issues Connection Service Assessment (identifies easements, main locations)
4. Design engineer prepares connection plans per TasWater standard drawings
5. TasWater approves designs
6. Connection works undertaken (either self-certified or TasWater contractor)
7. TasWater final inspection and sign-off

**TasWater Contact**: Devonport/Latrobe/Central Coast regions → Launceston Regional Office
- Phone: TasWater Customer Service (online portal or phone)
- Website: www.taswater.com.au

- Some councils publish standards on their website; request if not available

### Application in DA/BA
- Use approved standard drawings for pavement, kerbing, parking
- Any non-standard designs require engineer's justification
- Pavement design per AS 3798 aligned with council standards

## 8. State Growth Road Requirements

For developments accessing State Growth-managed roads (e.g., Bass Highway, major regional roads):

### Pre-Application Requirements
1. **Site Access Proposal** — Submitted to State Growth for review
2. **Sight Distance Assessment** — Minimum 90-120m sight lines (per road speed)
3. **Access Width** — Typically 6m for single-lane, 7m for two-way (on State road)
4. **Intersection Geometry** — May require traffic signals or roundabout design

### State Growth Approvals
- If on State road, State Growth consent required (in addition to council DA)
- Application submitted by designer or property owner
- Assessment 2-4 weeks typically
- Conditions may include pavement repairs, intersection upgrades, traffic analysis

### Access Design
- Design per AUSTROADS standards (Guide to Road Design)
- Sight distance calculations for design speed of road
- Heavy vehicle swept paths per AS 2890.2
- Staging of works (if road upgrades staged)

**State Growth Contact (Tasmania):**
- Infrastructure Tasmania (formerly State Growth)
- Phone / Email: check www.infrastructure.tas.gov.au for road-specific contacts
- Online application portal available

## 10. Common Planning Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Parking Shortfall
**Problem**: Development doesn't provide minimum parking per C2.0
**Solutions**:
- Performance criteria approach: On-street parking available, public transport accessible
- Shared parking arrangement (with adjacent property, off-hours use)
- Parking reductions for high-turnover uses (e.g., office vs. warehouse)
- Bicycle parking, car-share spaces in lieu of standard spaces

### Issue: Stormwater Detention Infeasible
**Problem**: Site too small or contaminated for on-site detention
**Solutions**:
- Underground storage (beneath parking, building slab if structurally sound)
- Connection to council network (if oversized main exists)
- Bioretention swales or rain gardens (vegetated filtration)
- Permeable paving (reduces runoff volume)
- Formal discharge agreement with council (payment in lieu)

### Issue: Noise from Industrial Operations
**Problem**: Noise from manufacturing, vehicles may affect neighboring residential
**Solutions**:
- Acoustic enclosure of plant/equipment
- Operating hour restrictions (e.g., no night shifts)
- Reduced vehicle movements during sensitive hours
- Noise barrier / vegetated buffer
- Baseline noise survey and ongoing monitoring agreement

### Issue: Heritage or Aboriginal Significance Triggered
**Problem**: Site in heritage precinct or Aboriginal heritage area identified
**Solutions**:
- Heritage impact assessment report
- Archaeological survey (if required)
- Design modifications to minimize impact
- Consultation with heritage authorities / Aboriginal groups
- Conditions on permit regarding unexpected finds

### Issue: Site in Flood Zone
**Problem**: Property in 1% AEP flood area; DA requires flood-resilient design
**Solutions**:
- Elevated slab above flood level + freeboard
- Flood-resistant materials below flood level
- Removable flood barriers
- Mechanical plant relocation above flood level
- Flood emergency plan

## 11. Tasmanian Building Standards and Certificates

### Building Act 2016 (Tasmania) — Key Requirements

1. **Building Surveyor Appointment**
   - BA must be lodged with a Registered Building Surveyor (council-appointed or private)
   - Surveyor assesses compliance with NCC 2022
   - Surveyor issues Building Permit (on approval) and Certificate of Completion (on finish)

2. **Building Permit**
   - Issued after BA approval
   - Permits commencement of construction
   - Conditions noted (inspections, reporting)

3. **Stage Inspections**
   - Footings: After excavation, before concrete pour
   - Frame: After structural skeleton complete
   - Pre-lining: Before plasterboard/insulation installed
   - Final: Before occupancy, all elements complete

4. **WHS Requirements**
   - WHS Plan required before construction
   - Site induction, hazard register, emergency procedures
   - Compliance with Work Health and Safety Act 2011

5. **Certificate of Completion**
   - Issued by Building Surveyor after final inspection
   - Confirms building complies with NCC and BA conditions
   - Required before occupancy
   - Registered with council property records

### Fire Certificate
- For Class 7, 8, 9 buildings: Fire Safety Schedule required
- Fire systems (alarms, exits, suppression) tested and certified
- Ongoing maintenance plan

### Occupancy Permit (if required)
- For certain building classes (public use buildings)
- Issued by council or Building Surveyor
- Required before public entry

## 12. Sustainability and Section J (Energy Efficiency)

### NCC 2022 Section J Compliance

For commercial and industrial buildings, Section J energy efficiency is mandatory. Three pathways exist:

1. **Deemed to Satisfy (DTS)** — Prescriptive compliance
   - Insulation R-values (walls, roof, glazing)
   - HVAC efficiency ratings
   - Lighting power density limits
   - No modeling required
   - Fastest approval pathway

2. **JV2 (Building Energy Rating)**
   - Whole-of-building energy modeling
   - Building modeled in simulation software (e.g., AccuRate, FirstRate5)
   - Target energy consumption (kWh/m² annually)
   - Flexibility in design (e.g., high-performance glazing offsets lower insulation)

3. **JV3 (Simplified Trade-Off)**
   - Component performance trade-offs
   - If one component exceeds requirement, another must compensate
   - Mid-ground between DTS and full modeling

### Tasmanian Climate
- **Climate Zone**: Zone 2 or 3 (cool temperate; varies by region)
- **Insulation targets**: R2.5-R3.5 walls, R4.0-R5.0 roof (varies by zone)
- **Glazing**: Double-glazed minimum; solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC) varies
- **HVAC**: Minimum EER 3.2 cooling, COP 3.5 heating (if heat pump)

## 13. Environmental Protection Authority (EPA) Tasmania

For industrial developments with potential environmental impacts:

### Triggering Events
- Discharge of wastewater to environment (not sewer)
- Air emissions (dust, odors, volatile organic compounds)
- Hazardous materials storage
- Noise exceeding EPA guidelines (>50 dB(A) day, >40 dB(A) night in residential area)
- Contaminated land disturbance

### EPA Approvals
- **Abatement Notice**: Required if pollution risk identified
- **Permit to Discharge**: For specific waste streams
- **Noise Compliance Certificate**: For higher-noise operations

### Documentation
- Environmental Impact Assessment (part of DA)
- Operational management plan (waste, emissions, noise)
- Baseline monitoring results
- Contingency plans for spills, leaks

**EPA Contact**: Tasmania EPA (online portal or phone 1300 ENVIRON)

## 14. Aboriginal Heritage Requirements

### Tasmanian Aboriginal Heritage Standards

- **Aboriginal Heritage Act 1975 (Tasmania)**
- Archaeological significance assessment required if:
  - Site known to contain Aboriginal sites (heritage register check)
  - Excavation or ground disturbance planned
  - Coastal or waterfront location (higher likelihood)

### Process
1. Heritage register search (online: Heritage Tasmania)
2. Desktop assessment by archaeologist (if indicated)
3. Consultation with Aboriginal community (through Heritage Tasmania)
4. Unexpected finds protocol (stop work, notify Heritage Tasmania)
5. Conditions on DA/BA if required

**Heritage Tasmania Contact**: www.heritage.tas.gov.au

## 15. Tasmanian Planning Scheme Transition Notes

As Tasmania transitions to the unified TPS (commenced 2023):

- **Legacy schemes**: Devonport, Latrobe, Central Coast may still have interim schemes in place
- **Transition period**: Councils will migrate to TPS progressively
- **Applicability**: Check with council which scheme applies to project site
- **New applications**: Typically assessed under TPS once available for area
- **Grandfather provisions**: Older approvals may be assessed under previous scheme

**Always confirm with council planning department which scheme applies to your project site.**

## 16. Typical DA/BA Timeline in Tasmania

### Pre-Application Phase: 2-4 weeks
- Title search
- Planning scheme check
- Pre-application meeting with council (optional but recommended)

### DA Lodgement to Approval: 4-8 weeks
- **Permitted pathway**: 28 days (no RFI)
- **Discretionary pathway**: 42 days + RFI responses (if needed)
- **RFI (Request for Further Information)**: 2-3 weeks to respond

### DA Approval to BA Lodgement: 4-12 weeks
- Detailed design (architectural, structural, civil)
- Geotechnical investigation
- Specialist assessments (energy, fire, traffic, environmental)
- BA documentation package assembly

### BA Lodgement to Approval: 3-6 weeks
- Building Surveyor review
- RFI responses (if needed)
- Approval and Building Permit

### Construction Phase: Variable
- Site preparation: 2-4 weeks
- Construction: 12-52 weeks (depends on building size and complexity)
- Stage inspections: Throughout (4-6 inspections typical)
- Certificate of Completion: 1-2 weeks after final inspection

**Total elapsed time from concept to occupancy: 6-18 months (typical range for light industrial)**

## Notes on Tasmanian Specifics

1. **Climate**: Cool temperate; significant wind exposure in North-West (wind speeds higher than mainland southern states). AS 4055 classification typically N3-N4.

2. **Flood Risk**: Northern Tasmania has high rainfall; flood zone mapping frequently updated. Always check current flood data.

3. **Heritage Areas**: Many towns have heritage overlays (e.g., Devonport historic precinct). Heritage impact assessment may be triggered even for industrial developments in certain locations.

4. **Bushfire Risk**: While less common in industrial zones, some rural/regional areas may be BAL > LOW. Check AS 3959 mapping.

5. **Aboriginal Heritage**: Significant Aboriginal sites throughout Tasmania. Desktop assessment recommended early.

6. **TasWater Services**: Not all areas have mains sewer/water. Remote properties may require on-site systems (septic, rainwater). Confirm with TasWater early.

7. **Regional Variation**: While this reference covers North-West Coast, southern and eastern Tasmania councils may have different interim schemes and transition timelines. Always confirm with specific council.
