# Australian Standards Reference for DA/BA Submissions

This comprehensive reference covers all Australian Standards relevant to commercial and light industrial Building Application submissions in Australia, organized by discipline and drawing type.

## 1. Structural Design Actions and General Principles

### Load Analysis Standards
- **AS 1170.0:2002** — General principles, safety levels, design process
- **AS 1170.1:2002** — Permanent, imposed and other actions on buildings
  - Used in: S200 (design parameters), structural calculations
  - Key sections: Unit loads, live load reductions, impact factors
- **AS 1170.2:2021** — Wind actions on buildings
  - Used in: S200 (wind class determination), C202-C203 (pavement design)
  - Key sections: Wind speed maps for Australia, exposure categories, pressure coefficients
  - Note: Replaced AS/NZS 1170.2:2011; applicable to buildings designed after April 2022
- **AS 1170.3:2003** — Snow and ice actions on buildings
  - Used in: Roof designs in alpine regions (less common in Tasmania, but required for mountain developments)
- **AS 1170.4:2007** — Earthquake actions on buildings
  - Used in: S200 (seismic classification), structural design parameters
  - Key sections: Importance levels (1-3), soil classifications, spectral acceleration

### Site Classification and Geotechnical Standards
- **AS 2870-2011** — Residential slabs and footings
  - Used in: Geotechnical reports, S201 (footing design)
  - Key sections: Soil classification (A, S, M, H1, H2, E, P), bearing capacity, footing depths
  - Applies to light industrial structures with similar foundation requirements
- **AS 3600:2009** (superseded by 2018) — Concrete structures
  - Used in: All concrete structural design, S205-S210 detail drawings
  - Key sections: Concrete grades, reinforcement design, durability classifications
- **AS 4055-2021** — Wind loads for housing (adapted for light industrial)
  - Used in: Wind-sensitive industrial structures
  - Key sections: Wind classification maps, importance levels, gust factors

### Corrosion and Durability
- **AS 4312:2008** — Atmospheric corrosivity zones (and outdoor exposure)
  - Used in: S209 (material specifications), structural adequacy certificates
  - Key sections: Corrosivity classifications (C1, C2, C3, C4, C5-M, CX)
  - Applied to: Steel frame designs, exterior finishes, fasteners
- **AS 2105:1997 / AS 2312:1995** — Guide to protection of structural steelwork against atmospheric corrosion
  - Used in: Protective coating specifications in S209-S210
  - Key sections: Paint system requirements, surface preparation, application standards

## 2. Concrete Structures and Design

### Concrete Design and Durability
- **AS 3600:2018** — Concrete structures (replaces 2009 edition)
  - Used in: All concrete design documentation, S205-S210
  - Key sections:
    - Concrete grades: N25, N32, N40, N50, N65, N80, N100 (strength classes)
    - Durability classifications: A1-A2 (internal), B1-B2 (sheltered), C1-C2 (exposed), D (aggressive)
    - Reinforcement design: cover to reinforcement based on durability class
    - Crack control, deflection limits, detailing
  - Frequency: Updated 2023; check project requirements for edition
- **AS 1379:2007** — Specification and supply of concrete
  - Used in: C209 civil notes, material specifications
  - Key sections: Concrete testing, quality control, supply requirements
- **AS 2758.1:2009** — Aggregates and rock for engineering purposes
  - Used in: Concrete and pavement design documentation
  - Key sections: Aggregate grading, testing, quality standards

## 3. Steel Structures and Connections

### Steel Design Standards
- **AS 4100:2020** — Steel structures (new edition, replaces 1998 version)
  - Used in: All steel frame designs, S200-S210
  - Key sections:
    - Steel grades: AS/NZS 3679.1 (300, 350, 450, 500+ MPa)
    - Limit states design methodology
    - Connections and fasteners (bolted and welded)
    - Torsion, lateral bracing, serviceability
  - Effective date: 2021; supersedes AS 4100:1998
- **AS/NZS 4600:2018** — Cold-formed steel structures
  - Used in: Light industrial sheds, purlins, cladding rails
  - Key sections: Cold-formed hollow sections, angle/channel sections, detailing rules
- **AS 1554.1:2015** — Structural steel welding, general requirements
  - Used in: S209-S210 weld specifications, inspection requirements
  - Key sections: Welding procedures, electrode types, inspection schedules
- **AS 4671:2001** — Steel reinforcing materials
  - Used in: Concrete reinforcement specifications in S205-S210, C204-C208
  - Key sections: Bar grades (250, 300, 500 MPa), mesh, wire, mechanical properties
- **AS/NZS 5131:2016** — Structural steel—Welded and bolted connections
  - Used in: Connection design, S204-S208
  - Key sections: Bolt grades, edge distances, capacity calculations

## 4. Timber Structures

### Timber Design and Construction
- **AS 1684.2:2010** — Residential timber-framed construction (Light timber frame—General construction)
  - Used in: Timber roof/floor framing designs, S203-S208
  - Key sections:
    - Timber grades and species
    - Roof bracing and wind resistance
    - Connection requirements (nails, bolts, straps)
  - Applies to: Light industrial buildings with timber-framed elements
- **AS 1720.1:2011** — Timber structures—Solid timber (general requirements and working stress design)

  - Key sections: Allowable stresses, moisture content limits, duration of loading, connection design
- **AS/NZS 1170.1:2002** — General principles (includes timber load duration factors)
  - Used in: Timing multipliers for loads affecting timber structures

## 5. Masonry Structures

### Masonry Design Standards
- **AS 3700:2018** — Masonry structures (major update, replaces 2011 edition)
  - Used in: Brick/blockwork designs, S202-S208, fire ratings
  - Key sections:
    - Mortar specifications and types
    - Unit strengths and grades
    - Wall thickness requirements and bracing
    - Control joints and movement allowances
    - Fire resistance ratings
  - Frequency: Updated in 2018; check for any 2024 amendments
- **AS 4455:2020** — Masonry units, pavers, flags and segmental retaining wall units
  - Used in: Material specifications for masonry, paving
  - Key sections: Unit dimensions, strength classifications, durability ratings
- **AS 4456:2020** — Masonry units, pavers and flags—Methods of test
  - Used in: Quality verification and acceptance testing
  - Key sections: Compression testing, absorption, dimensional tolerances

## 6. Fire Safety and Fire Resistance

### Fire Safety Standards (Part of NCC)
- **NCC 2022 Section C** — Fire resistance and stability
  - Used in: Fire Safety Reports, structural fire design (S200-S210)
  - Key sections:
    - Fire Resistance Levels (FRL): E, I, W ratings (e.g., 60/60/60)
    - Construction classification (1-10)
    - Smoke and toxic fumes limitations
  - Direct reference in BA documentation
- **AS 3959:2018** — Construction in bushfire-prone areas
  - Used in: Bushfire Risk Assessments (if BAL > LOW)
  - Key sections:
    - BAL ratings: BAL-LOW, BAL 12.5, BAL 19, BAL 29, BAL 40, BAL-FZ
    - Construction requirements for each BAL level
    - Openings, materials, non-combustibility requirements
  - Common for regional/rural industrial developments
- **AS 1530.1:2014** — Methods of fire tests on products—Ignitability
  - Used in: Material selection, fire safety specifications
- **AS 1530.3:1999** — Methods of fire tests on products—Flammability under non-piloted ignition
  - Used in: Cladding and internal lining selections
- **AS 1530.4:2014** — Methods of fire tests on products—Smoke and toxic combustion products
  - Used in: Interior finishing specifications

## 7. Plumbing and Drainage

### Water and Drainage Standards
- **AS/NZS 3500.0:2021** — Plumbing and drainage—Glossary of terms
  - Used in: All plumbing/drainage documentation (C205-C209)
  - Key sections: Definitions, terminology for water, sewer, stormwater systems
- **AS/NZS 3500.1:2021** — Water services
  - Used in: Water supply design (C205), fire services (if required)
  - Key sections:
    - Supply from mains, tanks, or bores
    - Meter installations, pressures, sizes
    - Backflow prevention
    - Fire sprinkler sizing
- **AS/NZS 3500.2:2021** — Sanitary plumbing and drainage
  - Used in: Sewer connection design (C205)
  - Key sections:
    - Sanitary drain sizing
    - Trap requirements
    - Ventilation and air admittance valves
    - Connection to council infrastructure
- **AS/NZS 3500.3:2021** — Stormwater drainage
  - Used in: C201-C209 (stormwater management)
  - Key sections:
    - Runoff calculations, rational method
    - Pipe sizing and grades per C204 compliance sections
    - Pits, junctions, invert elevations
    - Water quality treatment
    - Connection to council network
  - Critical for DA stormwater assessments
- **AS/NZS 3500.5:2021** — Roof water services
  - Used in: A104 (roof plan), C205-C209
  - Key sections: Gutter sizing, downpipe sizing, overflow provisions

## 8. Vehicle Parking and Access

### Parking and Traffic Standards
- **AS 2890.1:2004** — Off-street car parking
  - Used in: A101 (site plan), C202-C203 (parking layout)
  - Key sections:
    - Parking space dimensions: standard (2.6m × 5.4m), compact (2.35m × 5.0m)
    - Aisle widths and circulation
    - Ramp gradients and radii (max 1:8 or 12.5%)
    - Disabled access spaces (AS 1428.1 compliance)
    - Covered parking headclearances (min 2.4m)
  - Referenced in Tasmanian Planning Scheme C2.0
- **AS 2890.2:2018** — Off-street commercial vehicle facilities
  - Used in: Heavy vehicle turning movements (C202, C203)
  - Key sections:
    - Truck and articulated vehicle dimensions
    - Swept paths for common vehicles (e.g., 8.8m and 12.5m trucks)
    - Loading dock design
    - Intersection geometry
  - Required for industrial sites with heavy vehicle access
- **AS 2890.3:1993** — Bicycle parking
  - Used in: Site plans (A101, C202) if required by planning scheme
  - Key sections: Bicycle parking stand types, spacing, access dimensions
- **AS 2890.6:2009** — Off-street parking for people with disabilities
  - Superseded by references in AS 1428.1
  - Key sections: Accessible parking space design, accessible routes to building
- **AS 1742.2:2009** — Road signs, signals and line markings—Installation and maintenance
  - Used in: Site entry/exit design, traffic control signage
  - Key sections: Parking control line markings, directional signs

## 9. Access and Mobility (Disability Standards)

### Accessibility Standards
- **AS 1428.1:2021** — Design for access and mobility (Part 1: General requirements for access to buildings)
  - Used in: A101 site plan, A103 floor plans, accessibility assessment reports
  - Key sections:
    - Accessible routes (min 1.2m width, max 1:20 grade for ramps)
    - Doorway widths (min 800mm clear opening)
    - Toilet and shower design
    - Lift and stairs requirements
    - Disabled parking (min 3.2m wide + 1.2m access aisle)
    - Universal design principles
  - Mandatory for all buildings accessed by the public
- **AS 1428.2:1992** — Design for access and mobility (Part 2: Enhanced and additional requirements)
  - Used in: Specialist accessibility assessments
  - Key sections: Increased accessibility beyond Part 1 minimums
- **AS 1428.4.13:2021** — Design for access and mobility (Part 4.13: Accessible facilities)
  - Used in: Facilities design (rest areas, change facilities, first aid rooms)
  - Key sections: Accessible locker, shower, toilet, and change room design

  - Used in: WHS Plans, construction methodology

- **AS 2865:2021** — Confined spaces

- **AS 2550.1:2010** — Cranes, hoists and winches—General and definitions
  - Used in: WHS Plans for material handling operations

- **AS 2550.9:2010** — Cranes, hoists and winches—Powder-actuated tools

## Notes on Standard Editions and Updates

1. **2022 Amendments**: NCC 2022 replaced NCC 2019. All BA submissions should reference NCC 2022 unless transitional provisions apply.

2. **Recent Updates**:
   - AS 1170.2:2021 (wind loads) — Major update effective April 2022
   - AS/NZS 3500 series:2021 — Major overhaul of plumbing/drainage standards
   - AS 4100:2020 (steel structures) — Replaces 1998 version
   - AS 3700:2018 (masonry) — Updated fire resistance tables
   - AS 1428.1:2021 (access and mobility) — Reflects current DDA requirements

3. **Transitional Provisions**: Check with Building Surveyor if documents reference older standards (e.g., AS 2870:1996) for legacy buildings.

4. **Adoption by States**: While these are Australian Standards, individual states (e.g., Tasmania) may reference specific editions. Always confirm with local authority.

5. **Regional Variations**: Tasmania may have adopted different versions of standards compared to other states. Consult Tasmanian Building Surveyor for jurisdiction-specific requirements.

## How to Use This Reference

1. **For drawing schedules**: Identify the drawing type (A, C, or S series), then cross-reference primary and secondary standards.

2. **For specification writing**: Search for the standard in the discipline section, then reference the specific clauses needed.

3. **For compliance checking**: Use the cross-reference matrix to ensure all relevant standards are applied to each drawing.

4. **For design verification**: Start with the load standards (AS 1170 series), then apply material-specific standards.

5. **For regulatory submission**: Confirm all referenced standards are current editions by checking with the Building Surveyor and council.

This reference is current as of 2026 based on standards published through 2024. Always verify currency of standards with relevant authorities before finalizing submissions.
