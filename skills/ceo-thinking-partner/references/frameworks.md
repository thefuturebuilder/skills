## 1. Keith Cunningham

### Core Philosophy
Business is an intellectual sport. The CEO's primary job is to stop paying the "dumb tax" — the price of reactive, unthinking decisions. Replace fire-fighting with deliberate, high-level thinking.

### The Five Core Thinking Disciplines

1. **Find the Unasked Question** — The quality of your answers is determined by the quality of your questions. Most CEOs are solving the wrong problem because they never asked the right question.

2. **Separate Problems from Symptoms** — A symptom is what you notice. A problem is the root cause. Treating symptoms wastes resources and the problem persists. Three diagnostic questions:
   - What are the possible reasons I'm noticing this symptom?
   - What is NOT happening that, if it did, would cause the gap to narrow or disappear?
   - What IS happening that, if it stopped, would cause the gap to narrow or disappear?

3. **Check Assumptions** — Distinguish facts from the story you're telling yourself. Every decision is built on assumptions. Identify them, test them, and know which ones would invalidate the decision if they turned out to be wrong.

4. **Consider 2nd-Order Consequences** — First-order: What happens next? Second-order: And then what? Most people only think one move ahead. CEOs who think two or three moves ahead avoid the traps that catch everyone else.

5. **Create The Machine** — Develop executable plans and identify required resources. The business should run on systems, not on the CEO's personal intervention.

### Thinking Time Process
- Block 60 minutes (45 min thinking, 15 min capture)
- Dedicated space — same chair, pen, journal every time (ritual matters)
- Prepare 3-5 compelling questions before each session
- Eliminate all distractions
- Write continuously — every answer gets a new dot, keep going
- Best insights emerge in the "third third" after obvious ideas are exhausted
- Review notes within 20 minutes
- Capture top 3 strongest ideas
- Schedule follow-up sessions for worthy concepts
- Target: 2-3 sessions per week

### The Power of 3 — Decision Framework
Before any major decision:
1. What is the upside?
2. What is the downside?
3. Can I live with the downside?

If you can't clearly articulate all three, you haven't thought enough.

### The CFO Scoreboard
The CEO must intimately know three financial statements:
- **Income Statement** — Are we making money?
- **Balance Sheet** — What do we own vs owe?
- **Cash Flow Statement** — Where is the cash going?

Key principles:
- Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is reality
- Profits are a theory; cash is a fact
- What gets measured gets managed
- Good activities produce good numbers; bad activities produce bad numbers

**Critical Drivers vs KPIs:**
- KPIs are lagging indicators (historical). Example: revenue per customer
- Critical Drivers are leading indicators (predictive). Example: proposals sent this week
- Every business has only 3-5 critical drivers regardless of industry
- Focus the weekly scorecard on critical drivers, not just KPIs

**Rolling 13-Week Cash Flow Forecast:**
- Project cash in and cash out for the next 13 weeks
- Update weekly
- Identifies cash crunches before they become crises
- Forces discipline around cash management

### Building The Machine
The CEO's job is to work ON the business, not IN it:
- What systems are missing that force personal intervention?
- Where does the business break when the CEO is absent?
- What processes depend on tribal knowledge vs documentation?
- Who are single points of failure?
- What's the minimum viable system to solve each gap?

### The Dumb Tax
The price paid for reactive, unthinking decisions. Avoid it by:
- Never making a significant decision without Thinking Time
- Using Power of 3 on every major decision
- Maintaining a decision log and reviewing quarterly
- Building pattern recognition from past decisions

## 2. Tony Robbins

### RPM — Results, Purpose, Massive Action Plan
Not a to-do list. A thinking process.

- **Result**: What specifically do I want? (Clear, measurable outcome)
- **Purpose**: Why do I want it? (The emotional driver — this is what creates follow-through)
- **Massive Action Plan**: What actions will produce the result? (Flexible, not rigid)

RPM graduates complete 78% of strategic initiatives within one year vs 34% without structured implementation.

### Business Mastery Principles
- Strategic innovation, not just operational improvement
- Psychology drives execution — understand what motivates your team
- Financial optimisation is a system, not an event
- Sales mastery requires understanding human needs, not just features
- Sustainable growth requires all systems working together

### The 6 Human Needs (Applied to Business)
1. **Certainty** — Predictable revenue, stable systems
2. **Variety** — Innovation, new markets, product evolution
3. **Significance** — Market position, brand, competitive advantage
4. **Connection** — Team culture, customer relationships, partnerships
5. **Growth** — Scaling, capability building, learning
6. **Contribution** — Impact on customers, industry, community

A business that meets all six needs for its customers and team is nearly unstoppable.

## 3. AI-Era CEO Leadership

### The 2026 CEO Context
Based on research from IBM, Gartner, EY, World Economic Forum, and HBR.

**Core shifts:**
- AI is not a technology project — it's a strategic capability that reshapes every function
- What got you here won't get you there — think like a startup regardless of company size
- Balance incremental improvement with transformational bets
- Measurable ROI on AI initiatives is now expected, not optional (41% of executives cite this as top priority)
- Strategic alliances and joint ventures are critical levers for speed and flexibility (79% of CEOs planning these in 2026)

**Four AI Deployment Strategies:**
1. Focused Differentiation — AI to deepen competitive moats
2. Vertical Integration — AI across the full value chain
3. Collaborative Ecosystem — AI through partnerships
4. Platform Leadership — AI as a platform for others

**Workforce Reinvention:**
The CEO's job is to frame AI as an opportunity for the team, not a threat. Issue challenges worthy of people's creativity. Co-create the future rather than letting AI decide it.

## 4. Thinking Time Question Bank

### Getting Started
- How might I [action] so that I can [outcome]?
- What is the ONE thing that, if I got it right, would make everything else easier or unnecessary?
- What am I pretending not to know?
- Where am I paying the dumb tax right now?

### Strategy
- If I were starting this business today from scratch, what would I do differently?
- How would I run this business if 100% of future customers came by referral only?
- What's our unfair advantage and are we fully exploiting it?
- What market are we NOT in that we should be?
- What would happen to the business if I took 3 months off?

### Financial
- What are our 3-5 critical drivers and are we tracking them weekly?
- Where is cash leaking that we haven't noticed?
- What is our true cost of acquiring a customer and is it sustainable?
- If revenue dropped 30% tomorrow, what would we cut first?

### Team & Culture
- Who on the team is an A-player and what would it take to keep them forever?
- Where are we tolerating B-player performance and what's it really costing us?
- What would our team say is our culture if we weren't in the room?
- What system would make every team member 20% more effective?

### Competition & Market
- Who is my target customer? Who WAS my target customer? Who are my competitors' target customers?
- What will our industry look like in 3 years and are we positioned for it?
- Where are competitors investing that we're ignoring?
- What would a well-funded competitor do to disrupt us?

### AI & Innovation
- Where are we using AI as a tool when we should be using it as a strategy?
- What human capabilities become MORE valuable as AI gets better?
- Which of our processes could be 10x faster with AI and what would we do with the freed-up time?
- What new business models does AI make possible that weren't viable before?
